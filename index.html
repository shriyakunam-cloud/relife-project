Our Story

Bridging the Gap Between Science and People

Most people don't realize that everyday exposures — microplastics in drinking water, lead in aging paint, chemical runoff in soil — can quietly alter cognition, mood, and neurological development.

Research into these connections is severely limited. Resources for aspiring young researchers are scarce, and public curiosity is low. The ReLife Project was founded to confront exactly that — transforming overlooked science into accessible knowledge that protects real lives.

We believe every community deserves to understand what's in their environment and what it means for their health. So we bring that knowledge to them — in libraries, online, and through honest conversations with the experts themselves.

The Problem

Why This Work Matters

The science is clear. The public awareness is not.

💧

Microplastics in Water

Microplastics have been detected in drinking water worldwide and are increasingly linked to neurological inflammation and cognitive disruption.

🏠

Lead in Old Paint

Lead exposure — even at low levels — is well-documented to impair IQ, attention, and emotional regulation, particularly in children.

🌫️

Air Pollution & Cognition

Studies increasingly link long-term air pollution exposure to accelerated cognitive decline, depression, and neurodevelopmental disorders.

📉

Limited Research Access

Young researchers face enormous barriers entering environmental neuroscience. We're building bridges to change that.
